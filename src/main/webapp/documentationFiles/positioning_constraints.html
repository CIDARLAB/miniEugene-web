Positioning Constraints

Positioning Constraints restrict the location of the genetic elements' occurrences.
Constraint Description Examples
STARTSWITH α if α occurs, then α must occur at the first position STARTSWITH p
ENDSWITH α if α occurs, then α must occur at the last position ENDSWITH t
α AFTER β
α ALL_AFTER β 		If α appears in the design, then
all β components must precede all α components.

(BEFORE is equivalent to ALL_BEFORE) 		c AFTER r
t ALL_AFTER p
α SOME_AFTER β If α appears in the design, then at least
one β component must precede at least one α component. 		p SOME_AFTER t
α BEFORE β
α ALL_BEFORE β 		If α appears in the design, then
all α components must precede all β components
(BEFORE is equivalent to ALL_BEFORE) 		r BEFORE c
p ALL_BEFORE t
α SOME_BEFORE β If α appears in the design, then at least
one α component must precede at least one β component 		t SOME_BEFORE p
α NEXTTO β
α ALL_NEXTTO β 		If α appears in the design, then all β components must appear immediately before or after all α components
(NEXTTO is equivalent to ALL_NEXTTO) 		r NEXTTO p r ALL_NEXTTO c
α SOME_NEXTTO β If α appears in the design, then
at least one β component must appear immediately before
or after at least one α component		 p SOME_NEXTTO t
