Constraints

We categorize the miniEugene constraints into the following types of constraints: counting, positioning, pairing, orientation, and interaction.


Counting Constraints

Counting Constraints restrict the number of occurrences of genetic elements in a design.

Paring Constraints

Pairing Constraints restrict pair-wise occurrences of genetic elements.

Note! Eugene uses a zero-based indexing mechanism.

Constraint Description Examples
[i] EQUALS [j] the component at the i-th position must be equal to the component at the j-th position.

[0] EQUALS [1]
[i] NOTEQUALS [j] the component at i-th position must be different to the component at the j-th position.

[0] NOTEQUALS [1]
[i] EQUALS α the component at the i-th position must be the α component.

[0] EQUALS Promoter

[1] EQUALS rbs

Interaction Constraints

Interaction Constraints constrain (regulatory) interactions and relations among components in a genetic design.

Constraint Description Examples
α REPRESSES β If α and β appear in the design, then
the α and β have a REPRESSES relationship. 		tetR REPRESSES pTet
α INDUCES β If β appears in the design, then
the α INDUCES the β component. 		aTc INDUCES pBad
α DRIVES β If α and β appear in the design, then α and β have the same orientation,
α occurs upstream of β, and between α and β does NOT appear any terminator.
We say, α DRIVES the expression of the β.

Promoter DRIVES Repressor

pTet DRIVES GFP
